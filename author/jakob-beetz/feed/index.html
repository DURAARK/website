The DURAARK team have presented their results from joint efforts in WPs 3,4,5 culminating in D3.5 at the 2015 edition of the annual international EG-ICE (European Group for Intelligent Computing in Engineering). The workshop was attended by 40 participants from 11 countries.

Point cloud decompostion and association

A schema extension for the Industry Foundation Classes data model to accommodate point cloud data as a form of geometric representation of buildings models was presented. In order to cope with the large amounts of data usually handled in point cloud based workflows and preservation scenarios a serialization of IFC instances has HDF5 was presented.

Compression rates of Point Clouds in IFC

Both paper and presentation were well received and discussed extensively with other workshop participants, among which other papers focusing on point clouds were presented.

In the time between July 15-17, 2015 the 3rd annual workshop on Linked Data in Architecture and Construciton (LDAC) was held on the campus of the Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e)

The workshop was started by a number of presenations by the more than 33 partcipants from 12 countries.

One of the most important tangible outcomes was reaching consensus for a harmonized ifcOWL profile that serves as the foundation for the adoption as an official buildingSMART standard in October this year. This consensus was reached after extensive and constructive debates among the members of the group.

Additional information can be found in the the workshop report and the website of the workshop.

Together with colleagues from the Aalto University, University of Ghent, and AEC3, members of the DURAARK team co-organize the

(2 nd  LDAC  Workshop  &  6 th  eeSemantics  VoCamp)

to be held

May  26-­‐27,  2014
Aalto  University  and  Tekla
Espoo,  Finland

From the call for Participation:

“The  topic  of  this  event  is  why  and  how  to  use  the  Web  of  Data  technologies  for  building  information management.  The  interest  to  this  question  arises  from  the  expected  benefits  such  as  the  ability  to  easily  link building  information  models  to  and  from  external  data  sources,  to  open  the  models  to  new  use  cases  and applications,  to  enable  truly  decentralized  publication  of  models,  to  support  loosely  coupled  interoperation through  cross-­‐model  linking,  and  to  utilize  reasoning  and  other  services  developed  in  Semantic  Web  research. The  topic  is  studied  from  two  different  perspectives:  The  VoCamp  will  focus  on  use  cases  and  vocabularies  for the  Web  of  Building  Data,  and  the  LDAC  workshop  will  study  the  technical  solutions”
