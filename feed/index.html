CADQ-Q provided a Point Cloud consisting of 344 scans, which was automatically transfomed into an BIM model using the DURAARK IFC reconstruction component within Rhino/Grasshopper.

CITA members presented today the results of DURAARK in the “Reality Capture” seminar organized by CAD-Q Scandinavia.
The seminar was co-organized by FARO and addressed stakeholders from facility- and infrastructure management. In focus was the state of the art in laserscanning and related modelling tools. These offer an increasing amount of automation, predominately for the positioning of single elements.

The DURAARK presentation highlighted the research question and provided the participants with a nice outlook into future highly automated processes for the generation and query of semantically rich building models. A massive point cloud provided by CAD-Q served as demo for the DURAARK components.

November 2015 in the frame of the builingSMART day at the Danish Technological University . The event informed about the latest trends concerning Building Information Modelling, with a special focus on the needs of Building Owners, Architects and Engineers and Information exchange. A market place for ideas and discussions related to buildingSMART was the theme of the day, which was thoroughly discussed by all participants representing the entire knowledge chain of the building industry. The participants included developers, architects, engineers, contractors, manufacturers and personal from facility management.

The DURAARK team demonstrates, how the semantic web technologies, developed in DURAARK, allow to connect highly different building models, such as IFC and Point Cloud data. Visitors had as well the opportunity to check their internal organisation of building data in terms of longterm access, when they took part in a survey about sustainability in building data. Participants agreed, that thinking about long term access to BIM data is of immanent importanc
e for every stakeholder, that invests into these technology and processes.

Participants of the buildingSMART day showed a huge interest in the DURAARK project, especially about the latest advancements in the automatic generation of BIM files in the open IFC format from indoor Point Cloud scans. But there was as well a lot of interest in the archival strategies of DURAARK.

DURAARK thanks the organisers of this day –  especially Markus Lampe from DTU and Rikke Carlsen from bips.dk .

The increasing use of Building Information Modelling (BIM) challenges the way the building profession is handling the massive amount of building data. Technologies, which are used to access and retrieve information from the web, provide solutions to this problem. Especially semantic web approaches are of high interest here.
A workshop to these approaches and their benefit for building industry is given by a joint team from TU Eindhoven (Jacob Beetz & Thomas Krijnen), L3S Hannover (Stefan Dietze) and CITA Copenhagen (Martin Tamke and Henrik Leander Evers). The workshop ” Building Data and the Semantic Web” takes place n Friday the 11. December in the frame of the GeoBIM Europe conference in Amsterdam.

It and introduces participants to the general ideas of the semantic web, how these can create benefits for the building industry and finally how approaches developed in the EU project DURAARK support this.
We are excited to organize this event and invite practitioners to partake in the GeoBIM conference – http://www.geo-bim.org/Europe/agenda.html#friday-2015

The DURAARK team have presented their results from joint efforts in WPs 3,4,5 culminating in D3.5 at the 2015 edition of the annual international EG-ICE (European Group for Intelligent Computing in Engineering). The workshop was attended by 40 participants from 11 countries.

Point cloud decompostion and association

A schema extension for the Industry Foundation Classes data model to accommodate point cloud data as a form of geometric representation of buildings models was presented. In order to cope with the large amounts of data usually handled in point cloud based workflows and preservation scenarios a serialization of IFC instances has HDF5 was presented.

Compression rates of Point Clouds in IFC

Both paper and presentation were well received and discussed extensively with other workshop participants, among which other papers focusing on point clouds were presented.
