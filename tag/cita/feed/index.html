The other areas are  humanities and social sciences and medical and life sciences – as well with three nominees each. On November 9, the jury will announce the three winners of the Dutch Data Prize 2016.

Though we are excited to hear about the results we are happy about the Juries acknowledged for the unique and dataheavy collection of Point Cloud and BIM data of buildings, that we published on www.data.duraark.eu!

more info on the Dataprize:  http://www.researchdata.nl/diensten/dataprijs/
